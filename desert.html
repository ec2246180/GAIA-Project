The Desert

Explore the Desert

Deserts, often described as hot, dry, or empty, are not empty. They are home to various plants, animals, and organisms, and have been adapted to life in deserts for thousands of years.Deserts are arid areas with less than 25 centimeters of precipitation per year, often exceeding annual rainfall. They cover one-fifth of Earth's land and are home to around 1 billion people, making them one-sixth of the Earth's population.

Threats

Global warming causes more droughts,drying up water holes. Rising temperatures lead to more wildfires, changing deserts by replacing slow-growing trees with fast-growing grasses. Grazing animals can destroy desert plants and gold mining with potassium cyanide may poison wildlife.

Solutions

To improve dry areas, we can use water more efficiently. Rotating crops and planting bushes and trees can protect the soil. Leguminous plants help restore soil fertility by extracting nitrogen from the air.
